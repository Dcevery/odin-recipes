Recipe one: Tortillas

This recipe is abosulety the best thing i've ever had. Well, maybe.... To be honest, I've never made this recipe but look at that picture. It looks like the world best flour tortilla. Therefore, we must make this recipe in order to make the following recipe. With that said, we will begin with listing the ingredients and than the insructions.

  • 3 cups flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/3 cup of a neutral floweverd oil (extra virgin olive oil, vegetable oil, ect)
  • 1 cup water

So, now for the instructions...

  1. Combine the dry ingredients and mix together
  2. add the wet ingredients into the center of the mix and begin mixing until the ingredients are incorporated.
  3. Put the dough on a floured surface and knead until it is smooth
  4. Divide the dough into 16 pieces and flatten each piece with palm of your hand
  5. Cover and rest for 15 minutes.
  6. After the rest, roll out each piece and cook over medium heat. Don't stack uncooked tortillas because they will stick!

Recipe two: Carnitas

This recipe is VERY GOOD and I know this as a fact beacuse I have actually made it unlike the tortillas. I was dissapointed with all of the other carintas recipes I've tried until I found this one. So, let's started! Like the totillas, we will go over the ingredients and than the instructions.

For the pork shoulder. Quick note, you can use whatever dry rub you want but this is what I used.

  • 3 lb pork shoulder
  • 2 tbsp of salt
  • 2 tbsp course ground pepper
  • 2 tbsp of paprika
  • 2 tbsp yellow mustard
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp cumin

Next we have the ingredients for the braising liquid.

  • 3-4 dried guaillo peppers
  • 2 oranges
  • 1 red onion, roughly chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups water

So, now for the instructions.

  1. Preheat your grill! Start the pellet grill and set it to 225 F
  2. Cover the pork shoulder with a think layer of mustard and than liberally coat it with the dry rub.
  3. Place the pork shoulder in the grill for 4 hours
  4. After 4 hours, cut the pork shoulder into 2 inch cubes and place into dutch oven
  5. Squeeze the juice from the oranges and place them inside along with the other ingredients from the braising liquid
  6. Increase temperature of the pellet grill to 350 F and place the covered dutch oven into the grill
  7. Cook for 3 hours
  8. After 3 hours, remove the pork from the oven and crank the heat up again
  9. Let it heat up for 10-15 minutes
  10. Place the pork in a skillet and place it on the grill, break it apart and fry it up so that it is nice and crispy!
  11. THE FINAL STEP, throw that meat on a tilla' with some cheese of your choice, sals aka salsa, and all the other fixins. Eat it. Enjoy. You deserve it you King or/and Queen.
